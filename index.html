The Artist

Rooted in craft, guided by feeling.

Estelle began her artistic journey in 1975 with pottery, exploring wheelwork, hand-building, and mould-making as forms of creative expression.

In 1998, she transitioned into painting—initially to decorate her ceramics—but quickly fell in love with the medium itself. She started with acrylics and later moved to oils, which remain her favourite.

With the guidance of inspiring teachers Dennis Hilton Lees and Cyril Coetzee, she developed both confidence and a love for experimentation that continues to shape her work.

The Story

West Coast light, sea, and sky.

Estelle has been a member of the South African Society of Artists since 2012.

The sea has always been her greatest source of inspiration. Growing up in Durban and now living along the shoreline of Saldanha, she is constantly drawn to coastal landscapes and changing weather.

Plein air painting remains a passion—capturing fleeting light, shifting skies, and the many moods of the ocean. Colour plays a central role in her work, with hand-mixed tones that bring warmth, depth, and emotion to every piece.

The Studio

A space where the work comes to life.

This is where the paintings begin—surrounded by brushes, canvases, pottery, and the quiet rhythm of making. The studio holds both the story of Estelle’s beginnings in clay and her continued love for painting the sea.
